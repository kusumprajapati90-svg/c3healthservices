Understanding the Ryles Nasogastric Tube

Whether it is a paediatric Ryles tube for children or standard sizes for adults, professional insertion is critical for patient safety and comfort.

Ryles Tube Size & Usage

  • Adult Sizes: Generally 12Fr to 16Fr for feeding and Ryles tube drainage.
  • Paediatric Ryles Tube: Smaller French sizes (5Fr to 10Fr) tailored for infants and children.
  • Nasogastric Tube and Ryles Tube: Used for gastric decompression, medication administration, and enteral nutrition.

Ryles Tube Insertion Procedure

The procedure of Ryles tube insertion must be performed by a clinical expert to ensure the tube reaches the stomach and not the lungs.

  1. Measurement from nose to earlobe to xiphoid process.
  2. Lubrication and gentle insertion through the nasal passage.
  3. Confirmation of placement via pH testing or air auscultation.
  4. Securing the tube to prevent displacement.

Taking Care of Ryles Tube at Home

Flushing

Flush the tube with water before and after every feed to prevent blockages.

Positioning

Keep the patient at a 30-45 degree angle during and for 30 minutes after feeding.

Hygiene

Clean the skin around the nose daily and check for any redness or irritation.
